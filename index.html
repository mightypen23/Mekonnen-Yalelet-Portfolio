About

Mekonen Yalelet Getnet is an MSc graduate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Cassino and Southern Lazio, with a strong background in thermal and fluid dynamics, CFD modeling, and optimization methods. Passionate about advancing sustainable energy solutions, he excels in utilizing tools like ANSYS Fluent, COMSOL Multiphysics, and MATLAB to enhance heat transfer processes and optimize system performance. Mekonen combines academic rigor with practical experience from internships and research projects in solar energy and thermal storage systems. Energetic and curious, he enjoys exploring innovative engineering challenges and embracing multicultural environments, while also nurturing interests in languages and technology that complement his professional growth.

Research and Projects

Parametric Optimization of Plate Heat Exchangers for Zeotropic Refrigerant Condensation.

Current MSc research — UNICAS (in collaboration with University of Limerick)

  • Multi-objective optimization of PHE design parameters using MATLAB based NSGA-II algorism.
  • Aims to maximize overall heat transfer coefficient, minimize pressure drop [pumping power] and minimize effective heat transfer area[cost].
  • Pareto fronts were generated and TOPSIS is used as a decision-making tool.
  • Studied the overall system level effect of optimized PHE parameters on selected HTHP layout.

Experimental Investigation of PCM Integrated Indirect Solar Cooking system

MSc thesis — ASTU (2018–2020)

  • Employed the concept of conjugate heat transfer using COMSOL Multiphysics to simulate, characterize and optimize the heat and fluid flow behaviour of the heat transfer fluid and phase change material.
  • Designed and fabricated test rig for indirect solar cooking system.
  • Enabled extended period of cooking for 2hrs using thermal energy storage unit and increased overall system efficiency by 19%..
  • Numerically modelled and simulated the flow of HTF inside a solar collector using ANSYS Fluent software.
  • Validated the numerical result with RMSE<10%.
